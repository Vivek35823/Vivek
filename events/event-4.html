Who Was Swami Vivekananda?

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was a towering spiritual figure who brought Indian philosophy and spirituality to the Western world. Born Narendranath Datta in Calcutta, he became a principal disciple of Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa and later founded the Ramakrishna Mission. His powerful message of "Arise, Awake, and Stop Not Until the Goal is Reached" continues to inspire millions.
