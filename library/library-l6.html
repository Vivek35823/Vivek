On Caste

A Critical Examination of Social Systems

From the Archives of
Swami Vivekananda

Universal Perspectives on Society

This critical examination of the caste system and its social implications presents Vivekananda's progressive views on social organization and human equality. He challenges narrow perspectives and advocates for a more inclusive understanding of human worth and dignity.

"Every nation has a tendency to think that it is the greatest nation in the world. The same is true about individuals. This is very natural. But we must not lose sight of the fact that the world is composed of many nations and individuals."

Vivekananda's analysis reveals how all societies tend toward self-centeredness and exaggeration of their own importance. This observation applies not only to nations and communities but also to individuals who often overestimate their own significance while minimizing that of others.

The writing advocates for a more balanced and realistic perspective on human society. Rather than accepting rigid social hierarchies, Vivekananda encourages us to recognize the inherent dignity and potential in all people, regardless of their social position or background.

This timeless critique remains relevant today, reminding us of the importance of breaking free from limiting social conventions and recognizing our common humanity. Vivekananda's message is one of universal brotherhood and the transcendence of artificial divisions.
